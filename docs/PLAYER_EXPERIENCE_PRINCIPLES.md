# Player Experience Principles

## Funny in seconds

The player should understand the joke and the energy of the project almost
immediately.

## Responsive under chaos

Even when the screen is absurd, control trust and readable feedback must hold.

## Every run tells a story

Weapons, perks, enemies, and events should combine into memorable run-specific
moments the player can talk about.

## High readability beats raw spectacle

The game can be loud and ridiculous, but players should still know what is
happening and why they died.

## Shareable rivalry matters

Challenge links, scorecards, seeds, and ghost comparison should feel native to
the experience, not bolted on.
