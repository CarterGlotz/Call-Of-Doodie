# Soul

## Core promise

Call of Doodie should feel like a high-energy comedy shooter where the joke and
the gameplay both land. It should be instantly funny, mechanically responsive,
and replayable enough that a ridiculous run can still become a real mastery
story.

## Non-negotiables

- Humor comes first, but controls must still feel sharp
- The project must stay readable during chaos
- Mobile, desktop, and controller players should all feel welcome
- Runs should create memorable stories through perk, weapon, and enemy chaos
- The game should remain playful and absurd, not slide into generic grim combat

## Anti-goals

- Becoming a serious military fantasy
- Losing readability under effect spam and visual clutter
- Chasing realism at the expense of comedy and speed
- Becoming dependent on external assets that dilute the game's handmade feel

## Taste markers

- Fast, silly, celebratory, remixable
- Confident parody energy without needing mean-spirited tone
- Crisp feedback, exaggerated names, obvious run-defining choices

## Decision filter

When unsure, choose the option that best protects:

1. Funny first impression
2. Responsive arcade feel
3. Replayable build variety
